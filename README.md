<h1 align="center">Project Helion</h1>

<p align='justify'>
In the mechanics of our solar system, everything is defined by its relationship to the <b>Helion</b>—the solar center. The Sun’s gravity creates a "Hill Sphere," a region of space where it exerts absolute influence, keeping planets in stable, predictable orbits and shielding them from the chaos of deep space. Within this sphere, there is order; outside of it, there is only drift.


<b>Project Helion</b> applies this astronomical principle to academic integrity. Most digital learning environments are like open space—vast, unshielded, and prone to interference. Helion is designed to be the "Solar Center" for university assessments. It creates a high-gravity, controlled environment where recruitment tests and assessment workflows are pulled into a private orbit, isolated from the public catalog and the noise of the open web.


The goal of the framework is Stability. By restricting access and controlling the learner’s path, Helion ensures that proctoring isn't just an add-on, but the very force that holds the system together. It moves assessments from the "Aphelion"—the distant, cold, and unmonitored fringes—to the "Perihelion," where the light of observation is brightest and the integrity of the process is most intense.


This repository is dedicated to building that infrastructure: a framework where universities can host high-stakes evaluations with the confidence that their data, their tests, and their students are within a secure, governed orbit.


Helion exists to ensure that academic evaluation remains fixed, focused, and shielded by the light of integrity.

<b>True Center. Absolute Gravity.</b>
</p>
